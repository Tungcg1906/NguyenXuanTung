About me

I am currently based in Padova, working as a Junior Research Fellow with INFN and SciComp at RPTU, focusing on "Particle Detector Optimization with Deep Learning Techniques." I am a physicist and data scientist graduated at the University of Padova with Msc in Physics of Data.

Previously, I served as a laboratory research assistant in the Biophysics and Cell Biology Department at the University of Debrecen. There, I explored the expression of wild-type and mutant Kv1.3 channels in the CHO cell line, employing patch-clamp techniques and analyzing the resulting data. Additionally, I worked as a research analyst at the Neurochip Lab, where I examined neuronal recordings from in vitro neuronal cultures using multi-electrode arrays (MEAs). My research involved developing theories related to transfer entropy, coincidence analysis, and functional connectivity. Currently, I am collaborating with the SciComp team at RPTU on developing algorithmic differentiation for calorimeter applications at INFN.

I aspire to leverage my knowledge and skills to make a significant impact, aiding organizations in thriving in the digital era. My broad interests include biophysics, particle physics, and scientific computing, as well as the theory of computation, programming languages, and string theory. Additionally, I am deeply passionate about scientific machine learning, computer vision, and data science.
